The Netherlands consumer protection regulator launches an investigation into Roblox under the Digital Services Act (DSA) for not sufficiently protecting children

Hook

The popular online gaming platform Roblox, used by millions of children worldwide, is facing a formal investigation by Dutch authorities over whether it adequately protects its young users from potential risks. This marks one of the first major actions against a gaming platform under Europe's powerful new Digital Services Act.

What Happened

The Netherlands Authority for Consumers and Markets (ACM) announced it has launched an investigation into Roblox Corporation. The probe will examine if the platform is complying with the Digital Services Act (DSA), specifically regarding obligations to assess and mitigate risks that minors face while using its service. The ACM is acting as the designated national regulator for the DSA in the Netherlands.

The investigation focuses on whether Roblox has sufficiently identified and addressed systemic risks related to the protection of minors on its platform. The DSA, which fully came into force for large online platforms in February 2024, imposes strict requirements on \"Very Large Online Platforms\" (VLOPs) like Roblox to conduct rigorous risk assessments, implement mitigation measures, and undergo independent auditing. The regulator's concern stems from the platform's immense popularity with children and the potential exposure to inappropriate content, contact with adults, and manipulative design practices.

While the exact triggers for the investigation are not fully detailed in the public announcement, it represents a significant escalation of regulatory scrutiny. The ACM has requested information from Roblox and will determine if further enforcement action is necessary. The outcome could set a precedent for how child safety is enforced across the digital gaming and social media landscape in Europe.

Why People Care

This investigation matters because Roblox is not just a game; it's a vast, user-generated universe with social features, in-game purchases, and communication tools. It reports over 70 million daily active users, a significant portion of whom are under 16. Parents and child safety advocates have long expressed concerns about the platform's potential for exposure to adult themes, scams, and inappropriate social interactions, despite the company's safety features and moderation systems.

The move signals that European regulators are moving swiftly to flex the new muscles granted by the DSA. This law represents one of the world's most ambitious attempts to regulate the digital ecosystem, with non-compliance carrying potential fines of up to 6% of a company's global annual turnover. For a company like Roblox, which reported over $2.2 billion in revenue for 2023, the financial stakes are substantial. Beyond fines, the DSA also grants regulators the power to demand changes to a platform's fundamental design and operations.

This case is being closely watched as a bellwether for the tech industry. It tests how a platform built on user-generated content and real-time social interaction can balance creativity and openness with stringent, legally-mandated child protection duties. The investigation's findings could force Roblox and similar platforms to redesign core features, strengthen age verification, alter algorithmic recommendations, or increase transparency around their risk management—changes that could ripple through the global user experience.

Practical Takeaways

  • The DSA is Now a Live Regulatory Force: This investigation proves the EU's Digital Services Act is not just theoretical. Major platforms are now under active scrutiny, with child protection being a top enforcement priority.
  • Child Safety is the New Regulatory Frontier: Expect more investigations focused on how platforms popular with minors handle content moderation, privacy, dark patterns, and in-app purchases. Gaming and social media platforms are squarely in the crosshairs.
  • User-Generated Content Platforms Face a Tough Compliance Challenge: Platforms like Roblox, which rely on users to create content, have a uniquely difficult task in systematically assessing and mitigating risks across billions of experiences.
  • Global Impact from Regional Regulation: While the DSA is an EU law, changes Roblox might make to comply in Europe could affect its platform design and policies worldwide, demonstrating the \"Brussels Effect.\"
  • Parents Should Stay Informed: This regulatory pressure may lead to new parental controls or safety features on platforms. It's a reminder for caregivers to actively engage with their children's online activities and utilize available safety tools.
